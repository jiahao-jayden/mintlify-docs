### **2. Don't Limit Yourself When Writing Requirements**

Better questions to ask yourself:

- Is the content I want to express suitable for a website?
- What do I want visitors to see, do, and leave behind?
- Is this website more about showcase, interaction, distribution, or conversion?

You can even think one step further:

- If this idea were on a website, how would users engage with it?
- Beyond display, could this website also handle content, services, or data?
- Could this website continuously update, spread, and generate revenue?

For example, you could try:

- An interactive site with test result pages and sharing mechanisms
- A content site that continuously publishes around a niche topic
- A personal site that combines your experiences, works, and services
- A themed site built around brand story and product philosophy
- A display and booking site for physical stores, events, or courses
- A small tool site with user input, result feedback, and sharing
- A complete business site combining product showcase, content operations, and consultation conversion
